Brute Force Cracking Strength and Weaknesses
Strengths
  • Simple to initiate and only require time
  • Able to crack any passwords given proper time
  • Will work if security has no mitigation strategy

Weaknesses
  • Requires large amounts of computing power
  • Long and Complex passwords will take years to crack
  • If password requirements such as minimum characters and special characters are not defined, it will be difficult to crack the password

The core strengths of brute force cracking is the fact that it is simple to initiate, not requiring any information to begin a attack. It also will be able to crack any password given enough time, although this is also one of its detrimental weaknesses. Brute Force cracking can crack passwords ranging from milliseconds all the way up to years, on top of this it requires large amounts of computational power to brute force crack long passwords (8 Characters and up).

In conclusion Brute Force Cracking is easy to initiate, aims to go through all possible iterations of characters and requires time and computational power to achieve success.
