Brute Force Cracking Strength and Weaknesses
Strengths
  • Simple to initiate and only require time
  • Able to crack any passwords given proper time
  • Will work if security has no mitigation strategy
Weaknesses
  • Requires large amounts of computing power
  • Long and Complex passwords will take years to crack
  • If password requirements such as minimum characters and special characters are not defined, it will be difficult to crack the password

The core strengths of brute force cracking is the fact that it is simple to initiate, not requiring any information to begin a attack. It also will be able to crack any password given enough time, though this may seem like a formidable strength, it is in reality one of the reasons why people do not conduct brute force attacks. The time it takes for Brute Force attacks to crack passwords can range from anything between seconds up to days, months or years.

This is where attackers will make the decision between spending more time to crack a password, or to simply head on to the next one.
