How do Attackers use Dictionary Based Attacks?

One method of utilizing dictionary based attacks is by using an attack method called Password Spraying. Password Spraying in essence is to attempt a password simultaneously for multiple accounts at a time, in most instances password spraying would utilize a dictionary library to generate common phrases and passwords. Attackers tend to like this method due to its “scattergun approach” where they will only need to find one user who does have a strong password to gain access to their account or to the resource.

How these attacks work is usually by using scripts which will automatically access a login page of the attackers choosing and from there the attack would begin. The script would have a list of usernames that it can draw from and input into the username section, at the while the passwords are obtained from the dictionary library. The whole concept works out of the idea that there exists users who use very basic or easily guessable passwords.

Password Best Practices

Choosing hard-to-hack passwords and managing them securely may seem difficult or inconvenient. So here are some very simple and easy methods you can employ to lower the risk of your password being cracked through dictionary attacks.

Making hard to Guess passwords

  • Set passwords to be phrases to be easily remembered while being complex. E.g. “testisfordummies”
  • Avoid using single words, or single words followed with a number or special character like password1
  • Add in capital and special characters to increase its complexity
  • Employ the use of a password manager so you are able to have complex passwords while easily remembering and accessing the information
